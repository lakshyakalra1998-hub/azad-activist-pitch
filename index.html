In-house hot-section coating closes the only outsourced step in Azad's turbine blade production cycle. Removes external qualification dependency. Adds 300–500 bps EBITDA margin. Locks competitors out of the complete turbine component supply chain. Outlay: ₹80–120 Cr.
Vacuum Heat Treatment Expansion — CapEx
The binding throughput constraint on current turbine component capacity. Internal capex expansion at 3–4x lower unit cost vs. acquiring a standalone thermal processing plant. Immediate capacity debottlenecking unlocks deferred revenue on existing order pipeline.
PHASE 02 — DOWNSTREAM INTEGRATION (FY27–29)
Compressor Balancing Systems — CapEx + JV
Dynamic rotor balancing for both defense compressor rotors and hydrogen turbocompressors. The single highest-margin step in the hydrogen BOP supply chain. JV with an established metrology provider accelerates certification without greenfield timeline risk.

JV with a mid-tier European sealing specialist imports certification in 18 months vs. 4+ years for a domestic greenfield build. Unlocks SIGHT electrolyzer compressor trains as a complete system offering.
Electrolyzer BOP Module Assembly — Organic
Migrate from component supplier to module integrator for complete electrolyzer compressor trains. Revenue per project increases 4–6x. Margin structure shifts from component (20–22%) to engineered system (28–32%). No incremental capex — same precision manufacturing base, higher value-add position.
